# Retention Hook Writer

Write scripts that keep attention by stacking hooks across the whole piece, not only the first sentence. Use this for short videos, voiceovers, soft ads, Chinese creator monologues, product scripts, emotional scripts, and retention rewrites.

## Core Workflow

1. Identify the viewer's emotional entry point: fear, desire, identity, conflict, curiosity, or practical need.
2. Pick 2-4 hook types for short scripts, or 4-7 for longer scripts.
3. Place hooks at the opening, transitions, examples, payoff, and ending.
4. Write in spoken language. Make every section either relatable, useful, surprising, emotional, or specific.
5. End with a payoff, loop, question, or soft action.

## Retention Shape

Default short-video structure:

```text
0-3s: hard hook
3-10s: tension, problem, or promise
10-45s: value, story, proof, or examples
45-55s: payoff or memorable line
55-60s: loop, interaction, or soft CTA
```

For 2-4 minute scripts:

```text
0-5s: hard hook
5-25s: relatable scene or conflict
25-90s: point 1 + example
90-150s: point 2 + contrast
150-210s: point 3 + payoff
210s+: loop, interaction, or gentle CTA
```

## 12 Hook Types

Use hooks repeatedly. In longer scripts, add a fresh hook every 20-40 seconds when energy drops.

### Psychological Trigger Hooks

- **Suspense hook / 悬念钩**: withhold the key answer.
  - Use for reveals, mistakes, hidden reasons, or delayed payoffs.
  - Templates: "后来我才发现...", "真正的问题不是 X，而是...", "最关键的一点，很多人没意识到..."
- **Fear hook / 恐惧钩**: name a specific risk viewers want to avoid.
  - Pair it with a solution. Avoid fake panic.
  - Templates: "最危险的不是 X，而是...", "如果你还在这样做，很容易...", "很多人亏在这一步。"
- **Desire hook / 贪婪钩**: promise a gain, shortcut, comfort, result, or emotional reward.
  - Keep the promise believable.
  - Templates: "一个更省力的办法是...", "这样做，你会少走很多弯路。", "真正高回报的地方在这里。"
- **Identity hook / 认同钩**: make viewers feel seen.
  - Best for emotional, relationship, creator, career, and consumer scripts.
  - Templates: "你有没有遇到过这种人？", "如果你也是这种性格...", "这是不是很像你？"

### Content Format Hooks

- **Number hook / 数字钩**: give structure and expectation.
  - Templates: "三个方法...", "我总结了 4 个信号。", "90% 的问题都卡在这一步。"
- **Comparison hook / 对比钩**: show before/after, wrong/right, novice/expert, expensive/cheap.
  - Templates: "普通人会 X，高手会 Y。", "以前我以为 X，后来我发现 Y。"
- **Conflict hook / 冲突钩**: place two needs, views, or emotions against each other.
  - Templates: "他说得没错，但你还是很累。", "一边想要 X，一边又害怕 Y。"
- **Hot-topic hook / 热点钩**: connect to a current trend, product, debate, or widely shared situation.
  - Use only when relevant.
  - Templates: "最近很多人在讨论...", "为什么大家突然都在...", "这两天爆火的 X，其实说明了..."

### Action-Guiding Hooks

- **Urgency hook / 紧迫钩**: give a reason to act or listen now.
  - Templates: "趁你还没踩坑，先听这句。", "如果你最近正在 X，这个要早点知道。"
- **Interaction hook / 互动钩**: invite comments, answers, comparison, or self-identification.
  - Templates: "你是哪一种？", "评论区告诉我。", "你身边有没有这种人？"
- **Progress hook / 进度钩**: signal sequence and upcoming payoff.
  - Templates: "先说第一个。", "第二点更关键。", "最后这个才是重点。"
- **Easter-egg hook / 彩蛋钩**: promise a small surprise, final detail, or reward.
  - Templates: "最后有个小细节很妙。", "看到最后你会发现...", "最后这个办法有点幼稚，但真的有用。"

## Hook Stacking Patterns

Use combinations such as:

- Identity + conflict: "你有没有遇到过这种人？他说得没错，但你就是很累。"
- Fear + comparison: "最可怕的不是失败，而是你把市场反馈当成人格否定。"
- Number + progress: "这件事有 3 个处理办法，第二个最适合普通人。"
- Suspense + desire: "后来我发现，让关系变轻松的办法不是沟通更多，而是少找错人。"
- Hot-topic + identity: "为什么现在这么多人喜欢甜宠小游戏？因为现实里很多人真的太缺一句好好说话。"

## Chinese Spoken Style

For Chinese scripts, make it sound like a person talking, not a polished essay.

Prefer:

- Everyday openings: "你有没有发现", "你有没有遇到过这种人", "说真的", "你看", "对吧".
- Natural connectors: "其实", "但是", "那怎么办", "说白了", "时间久了", "你看".
- Concrete scenes before abstract ideas.
- Short paragraphs that can be spoken aloud.
- Slightly imperfect conversational rhythm.
- Practical turns after empathy: name the pain, then give 2-4 simple ways to handle it.
- Light colloquial humor when appropriate: "钢铁直男", "别跟自己过不去", "小嘴都可甜了".

Avoid:

- Essay tone, textbook explanations, and abstract openings.
- Phrases like "心理学里有个现象" unless the user asks for that style.
- Too many slogan-like symmetrical sentences.
- Grand truths that do not come from a concrete scene.
- Product pitches that arrive too early.

## Soft-Ad Rules

For soft ads, the product should feel like one possible relief, not the whole point.

Use this shape:

```text
1. Start with a common human moment.
2. Let the viewer feel "this is me".
3. Say the uncomfortable truth plainly.
4. Give a few useful coping ideas.
5. Bring in the product casually as a small comfort or option.
6. End with a light human line instead of a hard CTA.
```

Soft insertion templates:

- "有时候你不需要解决人生，只是需要一个能喘口气的小地方。"
- "不一定非要多认真，随便玩一会儿也行。"
- "它解决不了所有问题，但能让你轻松几分钟。"
- "别跟自己过不去，偶尔被温柔对待一下也挺好。"

## Quality Checklist

Before finalizing:

- The opening hook lands within 1-3 seconds.
- The script uses multiple hook types, not just one opening hook.
- The payoff matches the hook.
- The language sounds spoken aloud.
- Abstract claims are grounded in concrete scenes.
- Fear hooks are paired with solutions.
- Soft ads do not become hard pitches.
- The ending creates a loop, interaction, payoff, or gentle action.
